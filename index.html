Thursday 6 March 2026 — 17:00 GMT

Geopolitical

US-Israel War on Iran Enters Day Seven

The US and Israel continue strikes on Iran, with at least 1,332 killed since operations began on 28 February. Israel's military chief announced a "next phase" after 2,500 strikes with 6,000+ weapons. A panel of Iranian clerics is meeting to determine the regime's next leader after Ayatollah Khamenei was killed in airstrikes. The US House rejected a war powers resolution 212-219. Oil prices have surged 24-31% this week, with Brent hitting $91/barrel.

Dive deeper
The scale of the US-Israeli operation is now among the largest air campaigns since the 2003 Iraq invasion. The 6,000+ munitions deployed in seven days exceed the total used during the entire 2006 Lebanon War. The killing of Ayatollah Khamenei has created a succession crisis not seen since the 1979 revolution — the Assembly of Experts, a body of 88 senior clerics, must now select a new Supreme Leader while the country is under active bombardment. The failed war powers resolution in the US House fell largely along party lines, with only seven Republicans crossing over. Constitutional scholars warn this sets a precedent for extended executive military action without formal Congressional authorisation. The oil price surge is already rippling through futures markets, with some analysts projecting Brent could reach $100+ if the Strait of Hormuz is disrupted.

Iran Retaliates with Missiles on Tel Aviv; Lebanon Hit Again

Iran's Revolutionary Guards launched drones and missiles at Tel Aviv. Israeli airstrikes killed 123 and wounded 683 in Lebanon. Six US service members killed in a drone strike on a port in Kuwait. Qatar warns the war will force Gulf states to stop energy exports "within days". UK and France deploying naval and air-defence assets to the eastern Mediterranean.

Dive deeper
Iran's retaliatory strike on Tel Aviv used a combination of Shahab-3 ballistic missiles and Shahed-136 kamikaze drones, many of which were intercepted by Israel's Iron Dome and Arrow-3 systems. However, several penetrated air defences, causing casualties in southern Tel Aviv. The six US service members killed in Kuwait represent the first American combat deaths in the conflict, raising the political stakes domestically. Qatar's warning about Gulf energy exports is particularly significant — the country supplies approximately 25% of the UK's liquefied natural gas. The French deployment of the aircraft carrier Charles de Gaulle to the eastern Mediterranean signals a potential NATO-adjacent role, though France has stressed its presence is "defensive". Hezbollah's involvement remains calibrated: limited rocket fire into northern Israel without a full-scale ground mobilisation.

Ukraine Regains Ground; POW Swap Amid Stalled Talks

Ukraine captured more territory than it lost in February — net gains of 257 sq km this year. Russia and Ukraine exchanged 200 POWs each, but US-brokered peace talks remain stalled over territorial demands. Russia has suffered an estimated 1.27 million casualties since Feb 2022. 155 drones launched at Ukraine overnight including ~100 Shaheds.

Dive deeper
February's territorial gains represent a strategic shift: Ukraine is now using small, mobile infantry units to exploit Russian overextension across the 1,200 km front line. The 257 sq km recaptured in 2026 so far compares with net losses through most of 2024. The POW exchange — mediated by the UAE — involved mostly soldiers captured during the Kursk incursion. However, the broader peace process remains deadlocked: Russia demands recognition of its annexation of four Ukrainian oblasts, while Ukraine insists on the 1991 borders. The 1.27 million casualty figure (killed, wounded and missing) comes from UK Ministry of Defence estimates. Russia's overnight drone barrage of 155 included Iranian-supplied Shaheds, though Iran's ability to continue supplying these is now severely constrained by the ongoing US-Israeli strikes on its manufacturing facilities.

Global Bonds Suffer One of Worst Routs in Years

Bond markets are in turmoil as the Iran conflict drives oil prices higher and inflation expectations spike. The sell-off is hitting government debt across the US, UK and eurozone, with investors fleeing to commodities and cash. Analysts warn of stagflation risk if the conflict persists.

Dive deeper
The bond sell-off has pushed 10-year US Treasury yields up 35 basis points in a week — the sharpest move since the March 2023 banking crisis. UK gilt yields have risen similarly, increasing government borrowing costs by an estimated £4-6bn annually if sustained. The eurozone is particularly exposed: Italy's BTP-Bund spread has widened to 190 basis points, reigniting concerns about peripheral debt sustainability. The flight to commodities has pushed gold above $2,900/oz. Stagflation — the combination of high inflation and stagnant growth — last gripped the global economy during the 1970s oil crises. Central banks face an impossible trade-off: raising rates to fight inflation would crush already-fragile growth, while cutting rates risks embedding higher price expectations.

DRC Landslide Kills 200+ at Coltan Mining Site

Heavy rains triggered a landslide at a major coltan mining site in eastern DRC, killing over 200 including ~70 children. Pakistan-Afghanistan border conflict has displaced 118,000 people across both countries.

Dive deeper
The landslide struck an artisanal coltan mine in South Kivu province, where thousands of unregulated miners — including children as young as seven — extract the mineral critical to smartphone and electronics manufacturing. Coltan from the DRC accounts for roughly 60% of global supply. The disaster highlights the human cost of the global tech supply chain: major manufacturers have struggled to certify their coltan as "conflict-free" despite years of pledges. Rescue operations are hampered by ongoing armed conflict between M23 rebels and Congolese forces in the region. The Pakistan-Afghanistan displacement crisis, meanwhile, has overwhelmed UNHCR capacity on both sides of the Durand Line, with winter conditions making shelter provision critical.

Dive deeper

The NHS funding increase of 3% annually sounds significant but barely covers pay awards and inflation; the NHS Confederation warned it is "not enough to clear the 7.5 million waiting list backlog". The £1,000/year improvement in living standards is measured in real disposable income per capita and assumes inflation returns to the 2% target — an assumption now under serious strain from energy price surges. Capital spending on hospitals and schools was cut to fund day-to-day health spending.

Keir Starmer held calls with E4 leaders this morning on the Middle East escalation. The UK has moved naval and air-defence resources to the eastern Mediterranean. Emirates has resumed some Dubai flights, but widespread airspace closures continue to affect UK travel.

Dive deeper

Dive deeper
Gorton & Denton is a multi-ethnic, urban Manchester seat that Labour held continuously since 1935. The 25.6 percentage point collapse in vote share is among the largest in British by-election history for a governing party. The result suggests Labour is now losing votes in three directions simultaneously: to the Greens among young, progressive urban voters; to Reform among working-class communities; and to abstention among disillusioned 2024 Labour voters. Internal polling reportedly shows similar patterns across 60+ Labour-held seats. The Green Party's victory — their first ever in a parliamentary by-election — was built on local campaigning around housing, cost-of-living and opposition to the Middle East conflict. Senior Labour figures are now openly questioning whether the party's electoral coalition can survive to 2029.

US Economy Sheds 92,000 Jobs; UK Impact Expected

The US unexpectedly lost 92,000 jobs in February, rattling markets and raising recession fears. Analysts expect knock-on effects for the UK economy, particularly in financial services and export sectors, compounding the OBR's already-downgraded growth outlook.

Dive deeper
The February jobs report was a stark reversal from January's revised +125,000 gain. The losses were concentrated in government (-25,000, reflecting DOGE-driven federal layoffs), retail (-15,000) and professional services (-12,000). The unemployment rate rose to 4.4% from 4.1%. For the UK, the concern is twofold: first, US demand for British exports (worth £120bn annually) weakens in a recession; second, the City of London's financial services sector, which accounts for 12% of UK GDP, is tightly correlated with Wall Street performance. Goldman Sachs has raised its US recession probability to 35%, up from 20% a month ago. The OBR's 1.1% growth forecast for the UK assumed stable US demand — an assumption now in serious doubt.

EU Reset Lacks Strategy; Carbon Budget Fairness Warning

Dive deeper

The EU has secured British concessions on fishing access, youth mobility, and veterinary alignment — while UK demands on financial services equivalence and professional qualifications recognition remain unmet. MPs on the Environmental Audit Committee warned that unless the costs of decarbonisation are distributed progressively — with support for low-income households transitioning from gas boilers to heat pumps — public backlash could derail the entire net zero programme, as it partially has in Germany and the Netherlands.

Geopolitical

Iran War Enters Day Eight; Tehran Bombarded Overnight

Dive deeper
The "new phase" specifically targets government ministries, IRGC command centres, and communications infrastructure in central Tehran — a significant escalation from initial strikes on military and nuclear sites. Overnight explosions were reported near the Majlis (parliament), the Interior Ministry, and state broadcaster facilities. UNICEF's figure of 181 children killed is likely an undercount given the difficulty of verifying casualties in active strike zones. Trump's demand for "unconditional surrender" has no precedent in modern Middle Eastern diplomacy and has drawn criticism from European allies. The interim leadership council is reportedly split between hardliners who want to escalate retaliation via proxy forces across the region, and pragmatists seeking a ceasefire through Chinese and Russian mediation. Russia Providing Iran Targeting Intelligence on US Forces

Multiple US officials confirmed Russia is systematically feeding Iran satellite imagery and targeting data on American troop positions, warships and aircraft across the Middle East. Moscow is seen as seeking to prolong the conflict to divert Western attention and resources from Ukraine. The White House downplayed the intelligence sharing, insisting US operations are "completely decimating" Iranian targets regardless. The Chatham House analysis notes the war has exposed the limits of Russia's leverage, as Tehran's "partnership for peace" agreement with Moscow lacked a mutual defence clause.

Dive deeper
The intelligence sharing involves Russia's GLONASS satellite network and signals intelligence from electronic warfare units stationed in Syria. Sources say the data includes real-time positions of US carrier strike groups in the Persian Gulf and troop deployments at bases in Qatar, Bahrain, and Kuwait. The lack of a mutual defence clause in the Russia-Iran agreement means Moscow is not obligated to intervene militarily — and has not done so. This is a calculated strategy: provide enough support to keep Iran fighting and tie down US resources, while avoiding direct NATO confrontation. The Pentagon has reportedly adjusted operational security protocols, including changing communications frequencies and repositioning assets. Some international law scholars argue that Russia's involvement could constitute an act of war if US service members are killed using Russian-provided targeting data.

Pakistani forces claim to have seized 32 sq km of strategic Afghan territory. The UN reports 66,000 Afghans displaced, with 56 civilians killed including 24 children. The Taliban has signalled openness to negotiations, but Islamabad has rejected dialogue, insisting on an end to what it calls Afghanistan-based terrorism.

Dive deeper
This is the most serious Pakistan-Afghanistan military confrontation since the fall of Kabul in 2021. The 46 strikes hit TTP training camps but also Taliban government buildings, blurring the line between counter-terrorism and regime targeting. The 32 sq km of seized territory lies in the strategically important Spin Boldak-Chaman border corridor. The conflict risks a second major refugee crisis alongside Iran, with UNHCR warning its Afghan operations are funded at only 23% of requirements. Pakistan has already expelled over 1.7 million undocumented Afghan nationals since October 2023. India has expressed "grave concern" at the instability on its western flank.

Strategic petroleum reserves across the US, EU and Japan could cover about 90 days, but releasing them would only partially offset a Hormuz closure.
