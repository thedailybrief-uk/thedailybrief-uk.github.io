Evening Briefing

Thursday 6 March 2026 — 17:00 GMT

Geopolitical

US-Israel War on Iran Enters Day Seven

The US and Israel continue strikes on Iran, with at least 1,332 killed since operations began on 28 February. Israel's military chief announced a "next phase" after 2,500 strikes with 6,000+ weapons. A panel of Iranian clerics is meeting to determine the regime's next leader after Ayatollah Khamenei was killed in airstrikes. The US House rejected a war powers resolution 212-219. Oil prices have surged 24-31% this week, with Brent hitting $91/barrel.

Iran Retaliates with Missiles on Tel Aviv; Lebanon Hit Again

Iran's Revolutionary Guards launched drones and missiles at Tel Aviv. Israeli airstrikes killed 123 and wounded 683 in Lebanon. Six US service members killed in a drone strike on a port in Kuwait. Qatar warns the war will force Gulf states to stop energy exports "within days". UK and France deploying naval and air-defence assets to the eastern Mediterranean.

Ukraine Regains Ground; POW Swap Amid Stalled Talks

Ukraine captured more territory than it lost in February — net gains of 257 sq km this year. Russia and Ukraine exchanged 200 POWs each, but US-brokered peace talks remain stalled over territorial demands. Russia has suffered an estimated 1.27 million casualties since Feb 2022. 155 drones launched at Ukraine overnight including ~100 Shaheds.

Global Bonds Suffer One of Worst Routs in Years

Bond markets are in turmoil as the Iran conflict drives oil prices higher and inflation expectations spike. The sell-off is hitting government debt across the US, UK and eurozone, with investors fleeing to commodities and cash. Analysts warn of stagflation risk if the conflict persists.

DRC Landslide Kills 200+ at Coltan Mining Site

Heavy rains triggered a landslide at a major coltan mining site in eastern DRC, killing over 200 including ~70 children. Pakistan-Afghanistan border conflict has displaced 118,000 people across both countries.

UK Domestic

Spring Statement: Borrowing Down, Growth Forecast Cut

Rachel Reeves delivered the Spring Statement with borrowing down nearly £18bn — the lowest in six years. However, the OBR cut 2026 GDP growth from 1.4% to 1.1%. NHS day-to-day funding up 3% annually from 2026/27, though health leaders warn of "stretched budgets" and risk to elective care targets. OBR forecasts people will be £1,000/year better off after inflation.

PM Coordinates with France, Germany, Italy on Iran Crisis

Keir Starmer held calls with E4 leaders this morning on the Middle East escalation. The UK has moved naval and air-defence resources to the eastern Mediterranean. Emirates has resumed some Dubai flights, but widespread airspace closures continue to affect UK travel.

Labour Reeling After Gorton & Denton By-Election Collapse

Fallout continues from Labour's third-place finish in Gorton & Denton — a seat they won with 51% in 2024. Greens took it with 40.7%, Reform second at 28.7%, Labour collapsed to 25.4%. It was the Green Party's first by-election win. Polls show multi-party fragmentation accelerating across the UK.

US Economy Sheds 92,000 Jobs; UK Impact Expected

The US unexpectedly lost 92,000 jobs in February, rattling markets and raising recession fears. Analysts expect knock-on effects for the UK economy, particularly in financial services and export sectors, compounding the OBR's already-downgraded growth outlook.

EU Reset Lacks Strategy; Carbon Budget Fairness Warning

The Foreign Affairs Committee found the UK-EU reset lacks clear strategic priorities, with the EU achieving more progress on its demands. Separately, MPs warned the Seventh Carbon Budget must deliver fair benefits to all or risk losing public consent.

Morning Briefing

Saturday 7 March 2026 — 10:00 GMT

Geopolitical

Iran War Enters Day Eight; Tehran Bombarded Overnight

Iranians woke to massive explosions as US-Israeli strikes entered a second week with no signs of slowing. Israel announced a "new phase" targeting regime infrastructure in Tehran, while the death toll has surpassed 1,320 — including at least 181 children according to UNICEF. Trump has doubled down on demands for Iran's "unconditional surrender", ruling out any negotiated settlement. Iran's interim leadership council, formed after the killing of Ayatollah Khamenei, has pledged neighbouring countries will not be attacked unless strikes on Iran originate from their territory.

Russia Providing Iran Targeting Intelligence on US Forces

Multiple US officials confirmed Russia is systematically feeding Iran satellite imagery and targeting data on American troop positions, warships and aircraft across the Middle East. Moscow is seen as seeking to prolong the conflict to divert Western attention and resources from Ukraine. The White House downplayed the intelligence sharing, insisting US operations are "completely decimating" Iranian targets regardless. The Chatham House analysis notes the war has exposed the limits of Russia's leverage, as Tehran's "partnership for peace" agreement with Moscow lacked a mutual defence clause.

Pakistan-Afghanistan War Intensifies; Kabul Hit by Airstrikes

Pakistan has declared "open war" against Afghanistan after cross-border strikes escalated sharply. Pakistan Air Force jets struck 46 locations inside Afghanistan, including targets in Kabul city. Pakistani forces claim to have seized 32 sq km of strategic Afghan territory. The UN reports 66,000 Afghans displaced, with 56 civilians killed including 24 children. The Taliban has signalled openness to negotiations, but Islamabad has rejected dialogue, insisting on an end to what it calls Afghanistan-based terrorism.

Brent Crude Hits $93; Wall Street Posts Worst Week Since October

Brent crude leapt 8.5% to settle at $92.69, briefly touching $94 — its highest since September 2023 — as the Strait of Hormuz came to a virtual standstill. The Dow fell 453 points, the S&P 500 dropped 1.3%, and the Nasdaq sank 1.6%. Combined with February's shock 92,000 US job losses and unemployment at 4.4%, analysts warn of stagflation. Qatar's energy minister warned all Gulf oil and gas exporters may halt production "within days", sending European gas prices up 45%.

Ukraine Recaptures Territory; Peace Proposal 90-95% Agreed

Ukraine captured more territory than it lost in February — its first net gains since 2023. Ukrainian forces damaged two Russian Black Sea Fleet frigates at Novorossiysk and destroyed 111 of 141 Russian drones overnight. Russia's total casualties since February 2022 have reached approximately 1.27 million. Trump and Zelenskyy have agreed on "90-95%" of a peace proposal, though 70% of Ukrainians doubt the negotiations will deliver lasting peace. Russia continued strikes on Kharkiv and Kyiv regions, killing civilians.

UK Domestic

Petrol and Energy Bills Set to Surge as Oil Tops $93

With Brent crude above $93 and the Strait of Hormuz effectively closed, UK motorists face 10-20p/litre increases at the pump within weeks. Diesel has already hit a 16-month high according to the RAC. Heating oil prices are spiking sharply, hitting the 1.5 million UK homes that rely on oil. The energy price cap stands at £1,758 annually — 44% above pre-crisis levels — and while Ofgem has announced a 7% reduction from April, analysts warn the Middle East conflict could reverse that trajectory if Gulf disruptions persist.

Starmer Under Mounting Pressure Ahead of May Elections

Keir Starmer's approval ratings are the lowest of any PM in 50 years after 14 months in office. The Gorton & Denton by-election collapse — where Labour fell to third behind the Greens and Reform in a seat won with 51% in 2024 — has intensified questions about his leadership. The Mandelson-Epstein scandal forced the resignation of chief of staff Morgan McSweeney. Critical local and regional elections in May could determine Starmer's political future as multi-party fragmentation accelerates.

FTSE Braces for Turbulent Monday After Wall Street Sell-Off

London's FTSE 100 fell 1.2% on Friday and faces further pressure when markets reopen on Monday following Wall Street's worst week since October. Oil and mining stocks may benefit from surging commodity prices, but financial services and consumer sectors face headwinds from rising stagflation fears. The OBR's already-downgraded growth forecast of 1.1% looks increasingly optimistic as the Middle East crisis deepens. Energy debt stands at a record £5.5bn owed to retail suppliers.

Construction Sector in 14-Month Contraction; Job Confidence Falls

The UK construction PMI fell to 44.5 in February, down from 46.4 in January, marking the fourteenth consecutive month of decline. Confidence in job security among UK households has dropped to its lowest in six months, with the index falling to 92.6 in February. The combination of weak economic data, rising energy costs and global market turmoil is compounding the OBR's warnings that the Middle East conflict "could have very significant impacts" on the UK economy.

EU Reset Criticised; Voting Age Bill Advances Through Commons

The Foreign Affairs Committee found the government's UK-EU reset lacks clear strategic priorities, concluding the EU has achieved more concrete progress towards its demands than the UK. Separately, the Representation of the People Bill — which would lower the voting age to 16 and introduce automated electoral registration — cleared its second reading on 2 March. The government's independent review into foreign financial interference in UK politics is due to report by end of March.
