Badenoch Opens on Fuel Duty — “Why Raise Costs Now?”

The Opposition leader accused the Government of planning a stealth tax hike by withdrawing the 5p fuel duty freeze in September — just as oil surges past $100/barrel. Starmer insisted fuel duty remains frozen and will be kept “under review in light of what is happening in Iran.” Badenoch retorted: “The mother of all U-turns is him saying they are not increasing fuel duty.”

Davey Demands “Cast-Iron Guarantee” on Energy Bills

The Lib Dem leader warned household bills could rise £500/year due to the oil shock and asked Starmer for an unbreakable promise. The PM said he would “keep a close eye on it” but stopped short of a guarantee. Davey also attacked Farage and Badenoch as “Donald Trump’s biggest cheerleaders,” calling for a three-month zero-rating of VAT on heating oil.

Flynn Asks: Does Minab School Strike Constitute a War Crime?

The SNP Westminster leader confronted Starmer with the Amnesty-verified US Tomahawk strike on a primary school in Minab, Iran, killing over 170 people — the majority schoolgirls aged 7–12. Flynn asked directly whether this constituted a war crime. Starmer described the incident as “deeply troubling” and backed “full accountability” but stopped short of the term war crime.
