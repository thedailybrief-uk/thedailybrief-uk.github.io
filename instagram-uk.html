Consumer Confidence Hits Record Low

The GfK consumer confidence index fell to its deepest negative reading since records began, driven by fears over rising energy costs, job security, and the impact of the Iran conflict on household budgets. Every sub-index declined. Retailers reported the weakest footfall since the first Covid lockdown.

Energy Cap: April Drop, Then July Surge

Ofgem confirmed the energy price cap will fall 7% in April — brief relief before a sharp July increase as the full war premium feeds into wholesale gas prices. Analysts estimate typical bills could reach £2,200 by autumn, wiping out the April saving within months and pushing millions into fuel poverty.

Petrol Passes 150p Per Litre

Average UK petrol prices breached 150p per litre for the first time since the summer of 2022, driven by the Iran conflict’s impact on global crude markets. Diesel hit 158p. The RAC warned prices could reach 160p within weeks if Brent crude remains above $95. Rural areas are being hit hardest.

A public rift opened between the Energy Secretary and the Chancellor over new North Sea drilling licences. Downing Street refused to back either side, calling it “an ongoing discussion.”
