CPI held steady at 3.0% in February, above the Bank of England’s 2% target but in line with forecasts. Economists warned this is the last reading before the full impact of the Iran conflict feeds through to energy and food prices. Core inflation remained elevated at 3.4%, driven by services.

Sarah Mullally Enthroned as First Female Archbishop

The Right Reverend Sarah Mullally was enthroned as the 106th Archbishop of Canterbury at a ceremony in Canterbury Cathedral, becoming the first woman to hold the office in its 1,400-year history. She succeeds Justin Welby. King Charles attended; the Archbishop of York preached the sermon.

UK Business Insolvencies Rise as Energy Costs Squeeze SMEs

Company insolvencies in England and Wales rose 12% year-on-year in February, driven by soaring energy costs and weakening demand. The hospitality and construction sectors were hardest hit. The Federation of Small Businesses warned that without emergency support, a “wave of closures” could begin by summer.
