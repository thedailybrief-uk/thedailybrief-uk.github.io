Kent Meningitis Outbreak Reaches 34 Cases

Public Health England confirmed 34 meningococcal cases across Kent — up from 19 on Thursday. Two children remain in intensive care. Emergency vaccination centres have opened in Canterbury, Maidstone and Ashford. Officials describe it as the largest meningitis cluster in England in over a decade.

Energy Bills: April Cut But July Reversal

Ofgem confirmed a 6.4% cut to the energy price cap from April — saving the average household £117/year. But Cornwall Insight warns the July cap could rise £160, wiping out the saving entirely. Oil at $112/barrel and Brent futures above $115 are driving the reversal. The 1.5 million homes on heating oil face costs up 80% year-on-year.

Anti-Fascist Protesters Outnumber Far-Right at Holyrood

An estimated 3,000 counter-protesters surrounded a far-right rally of roughly 200 outside the Scottish Parliament. Police Scotland deployed 800 officers and made 14 arrests. The rally — organised under an anti-immigration banner — was the largest far-right gathering in Scotland since 2019. MSPs from all parties condemned the march.
